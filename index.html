Frequently Asked Questions

HDPE pipes are designed to transport water, gas, chemicals, and other fluids with precision, allowing for safe and efficient distribution in municipal, agricultural, and industrial settings. They are valued for their flexibility, chemical resistance, and 50+ year service life.

HDPE pipes offer superior corrosion resistance, lighter weight for easier installation, longer service life, lower maintenance costs, and better flexibility compared to metal alternatives. They are also more environmentally sustainable.

HDPE pipes can be joined using butt fusion, electrofusion, or mechanical fittings. Butt fusion creates a permanent, leak-proof joint as strong as the pipe itself. Electrofusion is ideal for field repairs and tight spaces.

We offer pipes from PN 2.5 to PN 16 pressure ratings. The rating indicates the maximum operating pressure in bar at 20°C. Higher PN ratings indicate thicker walls and higher pressure capacity.

Yes! Our HDPE pipes are certified safe for potable water transport and comply with IS 7634, ISO 4427, and other international standards. They do not leach any chemicals into the water supply.
